Case Note: THE SUPREME COURT OF APPEAL OF SOUTH AFRICA
Source Folder: 2017

# FIRAC Case Note: Pan African Mineral Development Company (Pty) Ltd & Others v Aquila Steel (S Africa) (Pty) Ltd 

**Neutral Citation**: Pan African Mineral Development Company (Pty) Ltd & others v Aquila Steel (S Africa) (Pty) Ltd (179/2017) [2017] ZASCA 165

### F – Facts

The case arose from a dispute regarding mineral rights pertaining to the Kuruman properties. ZIZA Limited (ZIZA), originally established as the Bechuanaland Railway Company Limited in 1893, held old order mineral rights, which were subsequently governed by the Mineral and Petroleum Resources Development Act 28 of 2002 (MPRDA). On 19 April 2005, ZIZA applied for a prospecting right, which was accepted by the Regional Manager of the Department of Mineral Resources (DMR) on 17 August 2005. 

However, on 2 May 2006, Aquila Steel (S Africa) (Pty) Ltd (Aquila) also applied for a prospecting right over properties that overlapped with those of ZIZA, and this application was ultimately granted in October 2006. ZIZA's prospecting right was later issued in February 2008, after considerable administrative delays and overlapping applications. 

Aquila contested the validity of ZIZA's prospecting right in a subsequent internal appeal, which the Minister of Mineral Resources upheld. This led Aquila to seek judicial review of the decisions made by the DMR and the Minister under the Promotion of Administrative Justice Act 3 of 2000 (PAJA), resulting in a High Court ruling in its favor. This triggered appeals from both ZIZA and PAMDC, as well as a conditional cross-appeal from Aquila regarding the lapsed status of ZIZA’s rights based on its deregistration on 9 November 2010, from which it was restored in October 2014 ([paras 1–10], [3], [31]).

### I – Issues

The legal issues raised in this case include: 

1. **Validity** of ZIZA's prospecting right application amidst multiple overlapping applications (whether ZIZA's application had lapsed due to non-compliance with statutory requirements).
  
2. **Administrative law principles** relevant to the acceptance of applications for prospecting rights under the MPRDA, specifically concerning the lack of timely notification of non-compliance under s 16(3) of the MPRDA.

3. **The interpretation** of the MPRDA's transitional provisions, particularly whether ZIZA’s old order rights continued to exist after deregistration and until the application was granted or refused ([paras 11–12], [24]).

### R – Rules

The relevant legal authorities and rules in this case included:

1. **Mineral and Petroleum Resources Development Act 28 of 2002 (MPRDA)**, particularly § 16 regarding the application for prospecting rights, and Item 8 of Schedule II concerning transitional provisions granting exclusivity to holders of unused old order rights ([paras 11–12], [16]).

2. **Promotion of Administrative Justice Act 3 of 2000 (PAJA)**, which governs the procedural fairness of administrative actions and offers grounds for judicial review ([paras 8]).

3. **Case Law**: Key precedents cited included *Agri SA v Minister for Minerals and Energy* 2013 (4) SA 1 (CC), which clarified the nature of old order rights and the necessary safeguards to prevent arbitrary deprivation of property ([para 12]).

### A – Application

The Supreme Court of Appeal analyzed the interplay between ZIZA’s and Aquila’s applications for prospecting rights under the MPRDA. The Court emphasized that when ZIZA applied for its prospecting right, it was backed by unused old order rights, which conferred exclusive rights to apply without concurrent applications being processed. The acceptance and processing of Aquila's prospecting application while ZIZA's application was pending violated the exclusivity granted under the MPRDA, effectively invalidating Aquila’s rights ([paras 15–18]). 

The Court further established that Aquila's claims regarding defects in ZIZA's application were misplaced, ruling that even if the application did not strictly comply with regulatory requirements, it sufficed for initial acceptance as the application fit the legislative purpose ([paras 20–22]). The Court rejected arguments based on procedural non-compliance as invalidating ZIZA’s prospecting right, articulating that such formalities did not warrant a return of the application, which would have implied existence and consideration by the DMR ([paras 24–26]).

### C – Conclusion

The Supreme Court of Appeal upheld the appeal by ZIZA and PAMDC, concluding that ZIZA's prospecting right remained valid at the time Aquila applied for its rights, and thus Aquila’s rights were invalid. The Court emphasized that procedural defects raised by Aquila did not affect the validity of ZIZA's application. Ultimately, the appeal was upheld with costs, and the decision of the High Court was set aside, reinstating the Minister's original decision. The implications of this ruling reaffirm the notion that the statutory framework under the MPRDA protects the rights of holders of unused old order rights, ensuring stability in the rights conferred therein ([paras 30–33], [34]).
