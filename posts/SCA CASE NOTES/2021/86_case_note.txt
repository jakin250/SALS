Case Note: THE SUPREME COURT OF APPEAL OF SOUTH AFRICA
Source Folder: 2021

### FIRAC Case Note: Deeps Betting Grounds (Pty) Ltd v Desert Palace Hotel Resort (Pty) Ltd and Another (142/2020) [2021] ZASCA 86

#### F – Facts: Relevant background and procedural history ([paras 1–10])

This case revolves around an appeal by Deeps Betting Grounds (Pty) Ltd (Deeps Betting) against an order granted by the Northern Cape Division of the High Court. The order, issued by Sieberhagen AJ, interdicts Deeps Betting from conducting any gambling activities on the premises at Erf 7195, Upington, unless it possesses a valid gambling licence issued by the Northern Cape Gambling Board (Gambling Board). Deeps Betting's application for leave to appeal against this order was initially granted on 31 January 2020 ([para 1]).

Deeps Betting had a conditional counter-application against the Gambling Board’s Chief Executive Officer, seeking to hold them in contempt of court for failure to issue a necessary gambling licence. The counter-application was dismissed by Dauds AJ, who also refused Deeps Betting's leave to appeal. Deeps Betting sought to raise its own appeal concerning this dismissal, alleging a right to appeal under the principles set forth in **S v Safatsa** ([para 12]).

The factual backdrop included Deeps Betting's dispute with the Gambling Board following an earlier settlement agreement that required the issuance of a licence for bookmaker activities at the disputed premises. Despite this agreement, the Gambling Board did not issue the licence, citing legal contraventions under the Northern Cape Gambling Act No 3 of 2008 ([paras 5–9]). Furthermore, Desert Palace Hotel Resort (Pty) Ltd (Desert Palace) filed objections to the rezoning of the premises, outlining their concerns regarding Deeps Betting’s activities ([para 9]).

#### I – Issues: Legal or constitutional questions ([paras 11–20])

Several legal issues arose for consideration by the Supreme Court of Appeal:

1. **Jurisdiction**: Whether the Supreme Court of Appeal had jurisdiction to entertain Deeps Betting's appeal against the dismissal of its counter-application by Dauds AJ ([paras 11–12]).
  
2. **Validity of Appeal**: Whether Deeps Betting could pursue an appeal against Dauds AJ's order without filing the required petition within a specified time frame as required by the Superior Courts Act 10 of 2013 ([para 11]).
  
3. **Lawfulness of Conduct**: Whether Deeps Betting’s operation of gambling activities on the premises without a valid gambling licence constituted a contravention of the Northern Cape Gambling Act and associated regulations ([paras 19–20]).

#### R – Rules: Legal authorities and precedents relied upon ([paras 11–15, 17–19])

The court referenced the **Superior Courts Act 10 of 2013**, particularly regarding the requirements for leave to appeal as established in § 17(2)(b). The court noted the principles from **S v Safatsa**, which outline the scope of appeals and the conditions under which new grounds can be introduced ([para 12]). Furthermore, the court relied on the Northern Cape Gambling Act, particularly §§ 78, 79, and 80, which stipulate the necessity of a valid gambling licence for conducting gambling activities ([para 18]).

Additionally, **Regulation 7(c)** of the Northern Cape Gambling Regulations specified that a licence shall not be awarded to premises located within a radius of 500 meters from schools or places of worship ([para 19]).

#### A – Application: Analysis of how the court applied the law ([paras 16–20])

In addressing the appeal, the court noted that Deeps Betting's reliance on the previous court order—mandating the Gambling Board to approve a licence—was misplaced, as the board had cited legal reasons for non-issuance. The court emphasized that the requirements under the Northern Cape Gambling Act and the associated regulations were clear: the absence of a legitimate gambling licence rendered Deeps Betting’s activities unlawful ([paras 16–20]).

The court also scrutinized Deeps Betting's failure to secure leave to appeal against Dauds AJ’s dismissal of its counter-application. The court determined that Deeps Betting's appeal was procedurally flawed, having not complied with the requisite timelines as specified in the Superior Courts Act, which ultimately undermined its appeal against the dismissal of its counter-application ([paras 11–15]).

#### C – Conclusion: Court’s holding, ratio decidendi, and implications ([paras 21])

The Supreme Court of Appeal dismissed both the application for leave to appeal against Dauds AJ's order and the appeal against Sieberhagen AJ's order, concluding that Deeps Betting had not been issued the necessary gambling licence and was thus illegally conducting activities on the premises ([para 21]). 

The decision further solidified the application of the Northern Cape Gambling Act, chastising Deeps Betting for not adhering to the legal stipulations required for lawful gambling operations. It establishes a precedent reaffirming that any gambling activities must strictly comply with statutory licensing requirements to avoid legal repercussions. This case is a crucial reference point for understanding the obligations imposed by gambling legislation in South Africa and the enforcement of compliance by regulatory bodies.

In essence, the court illustrated the importance of adhering to regulations governing gambling licenses and the legal repercussions of failure to comply.
