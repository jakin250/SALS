Brain Gear Investments (Pty) Ltd and Others v Buhle Waste (Pty) Ltd and Another (102/2023; 103/2023; 108/2023; 110/2023) [2024] ZASCA 168

The case involves an appeal against a decision by the City of Mbombela Municipality (the Municipality) regarding the approval of a change in shareholding of Sembcorp Silulumanzi (RF) (Pty) Ltd (Silulumanzi), a water services provider.

Supreme Court of Appeal 2024 [2024] ZASCA 168

F – Facts ([paras 1–12])

The case involves an appeal against a decision by the City of Mbombela Municipality (the Municipality) regarding the approval of a change in shareholding of Sembcorp Silulumanzi (RF) (Pty) Ltd (Silulumanzi), a water services provider. On 14 November 2018, the Municipality granted its consent to this change, which included the appointment of Brain Gear Investments (Pty) Ltd (Brain Gear) as a 28% black economic empowerment (BBBEE) shareholder, a requirement previously conditionally imposed in an earlier consent dated 28 June 2018 ([paras 1–2]).

Buhle Waste (Pty) Ltd (the first respondent) initiated a review application to contest this decision, arguing that the selection process was flawed and that the Municipality had abandoned its oversight responsibilities. This review was based on sections of the Promotion of Administrative Justice Act 3 of 2000 (PAJA) ([paras 13–15]). The case's intricacies revolve around adherence to procedural requirements outlined in the June 2018 decision, where the Municipality was to oversee the selection of the BBBEE shareholder ([paras 4–12]).

I – Issues ([paras 15–24])

The critical legal questions presented in this case include:

  1. Legality of the Decision: Was the Municipality's decision of 14 November 2018 lawful, considering the conditions imposed earlier in June 2018?
  2. Failure of Oversight: Did the Municipality neglect to fulfill its supervisory role, thus rendering the impugned decision reviewable under PAJA?
  3. Administrative Action: Does the Municipality's action constitute administrative action as defined by public law principles?
  4. Procedural Fairness: Were the due process and fairness requirements adhered to in the selection of Brain Gear as the BBBEE shareholder?

R – Rules ([paras 25–38])

The court's analysis was rooted in various legal principles, notably:

  • Promotion of Administrative Justice Act (PAJA): governed the review processes involving administrative decisions, emphasizing legality, rationality, and fairness in administrative action ([para 15]).
  • Administrative Action Definition: The definition of 'administrative action' includes decisions made by state organs that affect legal rights and are rooted in public power exercised in line with legislative frameworks ([para 38]).
  • Case Law: The case referred to precedents such as President of the Republic of South Africa v South African Rugby Football Union and Umgeni Water v Sembcorp Siza Water (Pty) Ltd, which set important standards regarding the scope of administrative action and public duty obligations ([paras 36–37]).

A – Application ([paras 39–46])

The court underscored several failures on the part of the Municipality related to its oversight and decision-making processes:

  1. Lack of Oversight: The Municipality demonstrated a clear neglect of its oversight role stipulated in the June 2018 decision. It did not participate in the selection of Brain Gear, which was essential to affirm the selection's legitimacy ([paras 40–42]).
  2. Rational Connection: The impugned decision lacked a rational connection to the information available to the Municipality. The selection of Brain Gear, which had no relevant industry experience, was deemed unacceptable. The court pointed out that the information provided to the Municipality about Brain Gear's capabilities was misleading ([paras 45–46]).
  3. Procedural Flaws: The court found that the process lacked transparency and fairness, as key representatives from the Municipality were not consulted during the selection, contrary to what had been stipulated ([para 42]).

The court's reasoning reinforced the principle that adherence to procedure is vital for the validity of administrative decisions, particularly when the rights of stakeholders are affected by decisions made in the public interest.

C – Conclusion ([paras 47–48])

The court upheld the review application by Buhle Waste and concluded that the Municipality's decision of 14 November 2018 was unlawful due to its failure to adhere to the procedural requirements set forth in the earlier resolution. It declared the impugned decision invalid and outlined mandates for the Municipality to reconsider its position regarding the change in control of Silulumanzi.

While the court set aside certain orders of the High Court as unnecessary (specifically those related to remitting the decision and addressing timing for reconsideration), the core findings remained that the Municipality had neglected its critical oversight responsibilities and that its decision lacked the necessary legal justification ([paras 48]). This judgment emphasizes the importance of procedural fairness and compliance with stipulated administrative protocols, which serve as fundamental ingredients in the legitimacy of governmental decisions impacting public and private interests.
