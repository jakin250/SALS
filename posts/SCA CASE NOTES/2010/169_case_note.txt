Case Note: THE SUPREME COURT OF APPEAL OF SOUTH AFRICA
Source Folder: 2010

# FIRAC Case Note on Manong & Associates (Pty) Ltd v City of Cape Town (457/09)

## F – Facts

Manong and Associates (Pty) Ltd (the appellant) is a civil, structural, and development engineering firm led by Mr. Mongezi Stanley Manong. The company reported a perceived pattern of racial discrimination in the allocation of municipal contracts by the City of Cape Town (the first respondent) and its predecessor, the City of Tygerberg (COT) ([para 1–3]). The complaints involved two specific projects: the Central Business District (CBD) development in Khayelitsha and the ‘Setsing project’ in the Free State ([paras 4–10]).

In the Equality Court, the appellant claimed its exclusion from these projects stemmed from a systemic policy favoring white-controlled firms. The complaints were substantially supported by allegations against Mr. Hendrik Barnard, a civil engineering technologist with the City, who Manong accused of conspiracy to exclude the company from municipal projects ([paras 8–10]). Ultimately, the Equality Court found some merit in the complaint related to discrimination in relation to Khayelitsha but dismissed the CBD claim ([para 17]).

The case led to contentious appeals concerning both discrimination and issues of costs. Following extensive proceedings, the Equality Court had initially refrained from imposing costs against the appellant, stating that it didn't want to deter legitimate claims under the Promotion of Equality and Prevention of Unfair Discrimination Act 4 of 2000 ([para 18]).

## I – Issues

The primary legal issues presented to the Supreme Court of Appeal were:

1. **Discrimination Claim:** Could the appellant substantiate its allegations of unfair discrimination based on race in the allocation of municipal projects by the City of Cape Town and COT? ([paras 3, 19])
2. **Costs Order:** Should the costs be awarded against the appellant in light of the unsuccessful discrimination claim? ([para 18])
3. **Misjoinder:** Was the Equality Court correct in upholding the plea of misjoinder concerning the Setsing project? ([para 17])

## R – Rules

The court's analysis was grounded in both statutory frameworks and common law principles:

1. **Promotion of Equality and Prevention of Unfair Discrimination Act 4 of 2000 (the Act):**
   - **Section 7(c):** Prohibits unfair discrimination on the ground of race, focusing on practices that maintain exclusive control by a particular race ([para 7]).
   - **Section 13:** Outlines the burden of proof, shifting to the respondent once a prima facie case of discrimination is established by the complainant ([para 54]), supported by precedents such as *Prinsloo v Van der Linde & another 1997 (3) SA 1012 (CC)*, which articulated the concept of differentiation in equality jurisprudence ([para 55]).

2. **Costs Decisions in Constitutional Litigation:** Drawing from *Biowatch Trust v Registrar, Genetic Resources 2009 (6) SA 232 (CC)* and other relevant cases ([paras 83-89]), the court considered the underlying principles for awarding costs, particularly in constitutional matters.

3. **Misjoinder:** The court reviewed the procedural legitimacy surrounding claims and the requisite parties involved in a legal dispute.

## A – Application

**Discrimination Claim:** The appellant's claim of racial discrimination was undermined by its failure to prove that its exclusion from municipal projects resulted from race-based discrimination rather than other legitimate operational concerns. The Supreme Court held that the Equality Court adopted the wrong approach by requiring the City to disprove discrimination rather than placing the burden on Manong to establish a prima facie case ([para 19, 54, 59]). 

Additionally, the evidence presented by Manong was characterized as unsubstantiated and marked by distracting irrelevant assertions rather than focused allegations. The testimony from witnesses, including Barnard, was deemed credible compared to the appellant's ([paras 22–24, 58]).

**Costs Order:** The court found that the Equality Court made errors in its costs order by not recognizing the inappropriate conduct of the appellant in the litigation process. Manong's behavior, which included producing excessive amounts of irrelevant documentation and making outrageous statements, warranted a reconsideration of costs. The Supreme Court highlighted that such conduct suggested the litigation was mercenary rather than purely in pursuit of justice under the Act ([paras 68–79, 92]). As a result, the court overturned the no-costs order from the Equality Court, imposing costs against the appellant for both the litigation and the appeal.

**Misjoinder Issue:** The court affirmed the decision of the Equality Court regarding the misjoinder related to the Setsing project, agreeing that no evidential link justified including Futuregrowth Property Development Company in the proceedings ([paras 17, 64]).

## C – Conclusion

The Supreme Court of Appeal dismissed the appellant's appeals regarding its claims of discriminatory exclusion from the CBD project and upheld the finding of misjoinder concerning the Setsing project. Consequently, the cross-appeal by the City of Cape Town was successful, leading to an order for costs against Manong and Associates, including those incurred during the appeal ([paras 1–5]). This judgment reinforces the need for substantive evidence when alleging discrimination while simultaneously addressing the procedural expectations and accountability for frivolous litigation in equality matters. The outcome serves as a reminder of the court’s commitment to both fairness in public procurement practices and the promotion of effective equality in South Africa, grounded in the rule of law.
